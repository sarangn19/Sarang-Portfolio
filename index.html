Case Study — 01

Second Brain

EdTech · Learning Platform

A personal knowledge management app for students — organising notes, flashcards, and learning paths in one unified space.

Problem Space
Students across disciplines struggle to connect information across subjects, leading to scattered notes and passive re-reading instead of active recall. Knowledge stays siloed, never forming a true second brain.
Research Methods
  • Contextual interviews (12 students)
  • Diary studies over 3 weeks
  • Competitive audit (Notion, Anki, Roam)
  • Affinity mapping & synthesis
  • Usability testing (5 sessions)
Key Insights & Outcomes
Users need friction-free capture first, structure later. Spaced repetition increased retention by 40% in prototype testing. Linking notes to flashcards was the most-requested feature across all cohorts.

EMI Flow

FinTech · Loan Repayment UX

Reimagining the EMI onboarding and repayment experience for a lending app — reducing drop-offs and building financial clarity for first-time borrowers.

Problem Space
First-time borrowers in Tier 2/3 cities faced 68% drop-off during EMI setup due to jargon-heavy flows, unclear repayment calendars, and trust deficits around autopay mandates.
Research Methods
  • User interviews (15 first-time borrowers)
  • Funnel drop-off analytics audit
  • Mental model mapping
  • A/B testing of onboarding variants
  • Trust & anxiety framework
Key Insights & Outcomes
Plain-language rewrites of legal copy reduced anxiety scores by 52%. Step-indicator pattern with reversible actions restored user control perception. Drop-off reduced from 68% to 21% in A/B test.
