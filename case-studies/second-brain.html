Second Brain: Designing Against Study Overwhelm & Inconsistency

Students preparing for competitive exams appear productive — they create study plans, use multiple apps, and spend hours studying. However, this effort rarely translates into consistent progress or real mastery.

RoleUI/UX Designer
MethodInterviews, Affinity mapping, Secondary research
ToolsFigma, Figjam

Problem

Students preparing for competitive exams appear productive — they create study plans, use multiple apps, and spend hours studying. However, this effort rarely translates into consistent progress or real mastery. Despite having access to tools like note-taking apps, flashcards, and AI assistants, students struggle to maintain an effective study system.

Research

Secondary Research (Indian Context)

Students preparing for competitive exams rely on a combination of tools like Notion for planning, Anki for revision, Forest for focus, Unacademy for structured learning, and ChatGPT for instant doubt-solving. While each tool is effective in its own domain, they operate in isolation and fail to support the complete learning journey. This fragmented ecosystem increases cognitive load, disrupts consistency, and prevents students from translating effort into meaningful progress.

User Interviews

Participants30

The goal of these interviews was to understand deep behavioral patterns, emotional triggers, and real study habits beyond surface-level survey data. What I explored: how students actually study daily, how they revise and retain information, what breaks their focus and motivation, how they measure progress, and emotional struggles during preparation.

Affinity Mapping

Planning ≠ Execution
Students invest time in planning (Notion, timetables), but struggle to act. "I spend more time organizing my study plan than actually studying."
Passive Revision is Common
Most students re-read or highlight instead of testing themselves. "I keep reading my notes again and again, but I still forget in exams."
Environment Strongly Affects Focus
Distractions vary widely: phone notifications, noisy surroundings, messy study spaces. "Even a small distraction breaks my flow completely."
Motivation is Emotion-Driven
One wrong answer → self-doubt. Peer comparison → stress. "When others are ahead, I feel like I'm already behind."
Tools Are Fragmented
Students switch between notes apps, flashcards, videos, AI tools. "I use so many apps, but nothing connects properly."
Revision Strategies Are Inconsistent
Students use mixed methods: flashcards, mind maps, teaching others. No structured system.
Key Insight

Students are highly active but lack direction and feedback. Their study journey is unstructured, emotionally unstable, and hard to measure.

Conclusion

The interviews revealed that the core issue is not lack of effort, but the absence of a guided, connected learning system that supports execution over planning, active learning over passive reading, and confidence over anxiety.

Final Problem Statement

Students preparing for competitive exams struggle to plan their daily study, revise effectively, and track meaningful progress, because their learning process is fragmented across multiple tools, relies heavily on passive methods, and lacks clear feedback on actual understanding. As a result, they experience cognitive overload, inconsistent study habits, and declining motivation, making it difficult to sustain long-term preparation and achieve mastery.

Ideation

How Might We (HMW) Questions

Based on the research insights, the following questions were framed to guide the design direction:

How might we guide students daily without overwhelming them?
How might we make revision active and effective?
How might we show real progress to keep students motivated?

Key Solution Ideas

Guided Daily Study Flow
Instead of asking students to plan everything, the system provides a clear daily path. Shows "What to study today", breaks syllabus into manageable tasks, removes decision-making.
💡 Solves: Overplanning & decision fatigue
Built-in Active Recall System
Replace passive revision with question-based learning. Converts notes into questions automatically, quiz-based revision, instant feedback on answers.
💡 Solves: Passive learning & poor retention
Smart Revision (Spaced Repetition)
System decides when to revise. Schedules revision automatically, prioritizes weak topics, repeats content at optimal intervals.
💡 Solves: Forgetting & unstructured revision
Motivation Protection Layer
Designed to support users emotionally, not punish them. Encourages after mistakes, tracks streaks without pressure, shows small wins.
💡 Solves: Low confidence & inconsistency
Mastery-Based Progress Tracking
Track learning, not time. Shows topic-wise understanding, readiness score, highlights weak areas.
💡 Solves: Illusion of productivity
Context-Aware AI Assistant
AI that understands your learning journey. Explains concepts based on your level, generates quizzes from your notes, suggests what to study next.
💡 Solves: Generic AI help & lack of guidance
Unified Learning System
Everything in one place. Connects planning, studying, revision, and tracking.
💡 Solves: Fragmented tools & switching fatigue
Core Idea
From "multiple disconnected tools" → to "one guided learning system"
